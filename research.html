  • Confirmation Bias: Seeking data that confirms pre-existing beliefs.
  • Availability Heuristic: Overvaluing easily recalled (often vivid/repeated) info.

  • Bandwagon Effect: Believing/doing things because many others do.

5. SWOT Analysis: Navigating the Infodemic Market

A SWOT analysis helps frame the strategic landscape for addressing the infodemic, considering human cognitive factors and technological influences.

Strengths (Internal Factors)

  • Human capacity for System 2 (analytical) thinking.
  • Growing awareness of misinformation issues.
  • Advancements in HCAI for detection/mitigation.
  • Established HCI principles for better design.

Weaknesses (Internal Factors)

  • Limited working memory capacity.
  • Pervasive cognitive biases.
  • "Brain drain" from digital distractions.
  • Difficulty scaling critical thinking education.

Opportunities (External Factors)

  • Demand for trustworthy information tools.
  • Potential for policy/regulation on platforms.
  • Integration of media literacy into education.
  • Collaborative efforts (tech, academia, public).

Threats (External Factors)

  • Rapid evolution of disinformation tactics (e.g., GenAI).
  • Engagement-driven platform algorithms.
  • Political polarization and echo chambers.
  • Low public trust in institutions/media.
